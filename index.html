Premier League Sim

Mowson and Olsson aid Swansea revival

12/02/2017

Swansea 2-0 Leicester

Swansea City moved four points clear of the Premier League relegation zone in 15th place after beating Leicester City 2-0 at the Liberty Stadium.

After a closely contested opening half an hour, central defender Alfie Mawson’s sweetly struck volley from Federico Fernandez’s cushioned header put Swansea in front on 36 minutes.

January signing Martin Olsson, a left-back, fired the ball through the legs of Kasper Schmeichel after a wonderful pass from Gylfi Sigurdsson in first-half stoppage time.

Islam Slimani, one of two substitutes for Leicester at the break, was denied by Lukasz Fabianski as Swansea held on, leaving the Foxes one point and a place above the bottom three.

Brady Strike enough to check Chelsea

12/02/2017

Burnley 1-1 Chelsea

A superb free-kick from Robbie Brady on his home debut for Burnley gave the Clarets a deserved 1-1 draw against league leaders Chelsea.

The visitors had the perfect start to the contest, with Pedro collecting a Victor Moses centre and slotting the ball past Tom Heaton in the seventh minute.

But Burnley drew level on 24 minutes as Brady's left-footed free kick found the top corner, and they had chances to grab the lead but Thibaut Courtois saved well from both Matthew Lowton and Andre Gray.

The result extends Chelsea's unbeaten run to five matches and they are now 10 points clear at the top of the table while Burnley remain in 12th, on the same number of points as Southampton and Watford.

Mane brings Liverpool first league win of the year

11/02/2017

Liverpool 2-0 Spurs

Sadio Mane scored twice as a blistering first-half display ended Liverpool’s five-match winless run with victory over Tottenham Hotspur.

The winger, who missed most of January on Africa Cup of Nations duty, opened the scoring after 16 minutes, finishing Georginio Wijnaldum’s through-ball.

Just 138 seconds later Mane had his second, robbing Eric Dier before scoring after Hugo Lloris had saved twice.

The contest was more even in the second half although Joel Matip headed straight at Lloris and Philippe Coutinho shot wide.

Liverpool’s first league win of 2017 puts them on 49 points in fourth, within one of Spurs, who remain second despite their nine-match unbeaten ending.

Spurs could see the gap behind Chelsea extend to 12 points tomorrow.

Martial at the heart of Man Utd victory

11/02/2017

Manchester United 2-0 Watford

Anthony Martial scored one goal and made another as Manchester United beat Watford to stretch their unbeaten league run to 16 matches – the longest since Sir Alex Ferguson retired.

It was all United in the first half and only Heurelho Gomes and poor finishing prevented them an early lead.

Juan Mata broke the deadlock on 32 minutes when he turned in Martial’s cross from close range.

Mauro Zarate forced David De Gea to save his 25-yard free-kick after the break but it was brief respite.

Martial doubled the lead when he took a Zlatan Ibrahimovic pass, cut in from the left and slotted home to give United their 2,000th Premier League point.
